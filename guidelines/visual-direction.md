# Aryze Visual Direction

## Aesthetic: Scandinavian Realism

The Aryze visual identity draws from the Copenhagen design tradition — functional, precise, and quietly beautiful. Nothing is decorative for its own sake. Every visual element earns its place.

**Reference aesthetics:**
- Scandinavian product design (clean, purposeful, material-honest)
- Nordic architectural photography (pale light, strong geometry, minimal ornamentation)
- Enterprise SaaS design systems (Stripe, Linear, Vercel) — professional rigor, not consumer playfulness

---

## Photography Direction

### Light
- Flat, pale, natural daylight — overcast Nordic sky quality
- Soft and diffused, not dramatic or directional
- No harsh shadows, no golden-hour warmth, no studio flash
- Cool-to-neutral colour temperature

### Environments
- Clean architectural spaces — concrete, glass, pale wood
- Urban Copenhagen / Northern European environments
- Minimal clutter — generous negative space in frame
- Financial and tech contexts: trading floors, server rooms, clean office environments

### People
- Natural and unposed — not stock-photo clichés
- Professional but not corporate-stiff
- Diverse but not tokenistic — just real people doing real work
- Avoid: suits pointing at whiteboards, people laughing at laptops

### Colour treatment in photography
- Desaturate slightly toward palette — images should feel like they belong in #F7F9FA world
- No heavy filters, no high contrast editing
- Colour temperature: 5500-6500K (neutral to slightly cool)

## UI & Product Visuals

### Screen mockups
- Device frames: clean flat mockups, no perspective distortion
- Background: #F7F9FA or #E3E8EB — never white or black
- Shadow: 0 8px 24px rgba(0, 30, 43, 0.12) — subtle lift
- Screens should show real, believable data — not placeholder lorem ipsum

### Data visualisation
- Chart colour tokens: #001E2B, #007599, #006282, #6FCF97, #B8C1C7
- Chart styles: clean, minimal axes, no heavy gridlines
- No 3D charts — always flat
- Label legibility takes priority over decorative styling

### Illustration style (if used)
- Flat, geometric, minimal — consistent with Scandinavian aesthetic
- Restricted to brand palette
- Lines, shapes, and abstract forms — not character illustration
- Use sparingly — Aryze defaults to real photography and clean UI over illustration

---

## Layout Principles

### Hierarchy
1. One dominant visual element per screen or section — never compete
2. Typography-led — Aryze layouts rely on strong typographic hierarchy, not visual decoration
3. Data and proof — show real numbers, real outcomes where possible

### Grid
- 12-column grid, 24px gutters
- Max content width: 1280px
- Section padding: 64px vertical (desktop), 48px (mobile)
- Cards in grids: typically 3-up (desktop), 2-up (tablet), 1-up (mobile)

### Whitespace
- Generous — the Scandinavian aesthetic requires breathing room
- Resist the urge to fill empty space with content
- Padding inside cards: always 24px minimum

### Visual Weight
- #001E2B carries the heaviest visual weight — use for primary headings and key UI
- #00AFE7 draws the eye — use only for CTAs and key interactive elements
- Everything else should recede — pale, soft, unobtrusive

---

## Pain/Solution Visual Pattern

When depicting problems vs. solutions in marketing materials:

- Problem state: Cool grey tones, #E3E8EB backgrounds, subtle red accents (#DC2626) — friction, complexity
- Solution state: Aryze palette, #00AFE7 and #D9F5EA accents — clarity, speed, resolution

This creates a visual before/after language without needing words to explain it.

---

## What Aryze Visuals Are Not

- Not playful or consumer-facing
- Not gradient-heavy or colourful
- Not dark mode by default (light-first, dark surfaces are accents)
- Not icon-dense or illustration-heavy
- Not cluttered — every element on screen must have a reason to exist
