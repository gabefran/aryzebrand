# Aryze Messaging & Tone of Voice

## Brand Voice

**Precise. Confident. Understated.**

Aryze communicates like a senior operator, not a startup. The voice is authoritative without being arrogant, technical without being inaccessible, and direct without being blunt.

Think: a Copenhagen CFO explaining a better way to move money. Not a Silicon Valley pitch deck.

---

## Tone Principles

### 1. Operational, not aspirational
Lead with what Aryze does and what it enables — not vague promises about "the future of finance."

✅ "Settle cross-border payments in seconds, not days."
❌ "We're reimagining the future of global money movement."

### 2. Outcome-first
Lead with the merchant or operator outcome, not the technology feature.

✅ "Reduce checkout drop-off. Increase completed payments."
❌ "Our A2A infrastructure uses direct bank-to-bank rails."

### 3. Conversion and drop-off framing (not cost framing)
When describing payment problems, lead with conversion loss and drop-off — not cost assumptions. Don't presume what a prospect pays or what their setup looks like.

✅ "Payment friction costs you completed transactions."
❌ "You're paying too much in card fees."
❌ "Your current processor is expensive."

### 4. Confident, not pushy
Aryze doesn't oversell. State the capability, let it stand.

✅ "Pay by Bank. Built for high-risk merchants."
❌ "The most powerful Pay by Bank solution on the market today!"

### 5. Partner-aware
Mastercard is a partner. Never position card payments as the enemy or the problem.

✅ "Built on Mastercard payment rails."
✅ "An alternative payment path alongside card."
❌ "Ditch card fees."
❌ "Cards are expensive and outdated."
❌ "The trust and infrastructure of Mastercard." (too presumptuous — use "built on Mastercard payment rails")

## Audience Personas

### iGaming Operators
Pain: High card decline rates, chargeback exposure, PSP restrictions, deposit friction.
What they care about: Deposit completion rate, compliance, speed of settlement, PSP relationships.
Tone: Direct, regulatory-aware, no fluff. They've heard every pitch.

### Crypto Exchanges
Pain: Banking access, fiat on/off ramp friction, cross-border settlement delays, compliance overhead.
What they care about: Speed, reliability, regulatory coverage, stablecoin infrastructure.
Tone: Technical peer — they understand infrastructure. Speak with precision.

### Digital Marketplaces
Pain: Payout delays, FX costs, multi-currency complexity, seller experience.
What they care about: Payout speed, FX rates, platform reliability.
Tone: Practical and ROI-focused.

## Key Messages by Use Case

### Chargebacks
"Chargebacks don't exist in A2A. Push payments are irrevocable by design — eliminating the dispute cycle entirely."

### Card Fees / Alternative Payments
"Add Pay by Bank as a payment path alongside card. Give customers choice. Reduce friction at checkout."
(Note: never frame this as replacing card or attacking card costs)

### Cross-Border Settlement
"Move money across borders at interbank rates. No correspondent banking delays. Settlement in seconds."

### iGaming Deposits
"Higher deposit completion. Lower decline rates. Built for the compliance requirements of regulated gambling markets."

## Copy Do / Don't

| Don't say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "Trust and infrastructure of Mastercard" | "Built on Mastercard payment rails" |
| "Ditch card fees" | "Add Pay by Bank alongside card" |
| "Your current payment setup is costing you" | "Payment friction costs you completed transactions" |
| "We're the best/fastest/most powerful" | State the specific capability, let it speak |
| "Revolutionary" / "Disruptive" / "Game-changing" | Specific, measurable outcomes |
| Emoji in professional copy | None — Aryze copy uses no emoji |
